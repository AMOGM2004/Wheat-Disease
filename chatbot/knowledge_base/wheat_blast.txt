DISEASE: Wheat Blast
PATHOGEN: Magnaporthe oryzae Triticum (MoT)
STAGE: Reproductive (heading to grain fill)

SYMPTOMS:
- Bleached or whitish spikelets starting from the tip of the ear
- Partially or fully bleached heads while rest of plant stays green
- Dark brown lesions at base of the rachis
- Shriveled, discolored, or absent grains
- Black sooty spore masses in humid conditions
- "Frosted" appearance at spike tip

CAUSES:
- Fungus Magnaporthe oryzae pathotype Triticum
- Spreads via airborne spores over long distances
- Warm temperature 25-30°C with high humidity above 90%
- Dense crop canopy traps moisture
- Infected seeds carry and spread the pathogen

TREATMENT:
- Apply triazole fungicides (tebuconazole, propiconazole) at heading
- Strobilurin fungicides (azoxystrobin) in combination
- Spray at flag leaf emergence and again at 50% heading
- Remove and destroy infected plant material immediately
- Treat seeds with systemic fungicides before sowing

PREVENTION:
- Use certified disease-free seed
- Avoid sowing during high humidity and warm temperatures
- Maintain proper plant spacing for air circulation
- Monitor fields regularly from heading stage
- Follow crop rotation in blast-prone areas

SEVERITY: High — can cause 10-100% yield loss
FAVORABLE CONDITIONS: Temperature 25-30°C, Humidity above 90%, Rain at heading
