DISEASE: Yellow Rust (Stripe Rust)
PATHOGEN: Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici
STAGE: All stages, most damaging at flag leaf

SYMPTOMS:
- Bright yellow to lemon-yellow pustules in stripes along leaves
- Pustules run parallel to leaf veins — stripe pattern is diagnostic
- Young pustules surrounded by pale yellow halo
- Infected leaves turn yellow then brown and die
- Yellow powder visible on hands when touching infected leaves
- Black pustules appear late in season

CAUSES:
- Obligate fungal pathogen Puccinia striiformis
- Cool temperatures 10-15°C with high moisture (dew, rain, fog)
- Spores spread by wind over hundreds of kilometers
- Dense sowing traps moisture and promotes spread
- New virulent races can overcome previously resistant varieties

TREATMENT:
- Propiconazole 0.1% foliar spray at first sign — most effective
- Tebuconazole or triadimefon also effective
- Apply early — once 5% leaf area is infected, damage is occurring
- Two sprays: at tillering and again at flag leaf emergence
- Strobilurin fungicides give good residual protection

PREVENTION:
- Grow resistant or tolerant varieties — most cost-effective control
- Avoid excessive nitrogen which promotes soft leaf tissue
- Monitor fields from tillering, especially in cool moist weather
- Remove volunteer wheat plants between seasons
- Early sowing helps crop escape peak rust pressure

SEVERITY: High — can cause 40-70% yield loss in susceptible varieties
FAVORABLE CONDITIONS: Temperature 10-15°C, Humidity above 95%, Cool nights with dew
